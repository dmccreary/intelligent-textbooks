    Sponsorship Plan: Sustaining Open Intelligent Textbooks

    Why Sponsorship?

    This plan outlines a practical, values-aligned approach to sponsorship that keeps every textbook free and open while covering the real costs of creating them.

    Executive Summary

    The Intelligent Textbooks project is committed to making high-quality education accessible to all through free, open-source textbooks. Creating these textbooks has real costs: AI image generation for covers and diagrams, API calls for content generation, and the time required to maintain 70+ case study textbooks.

    Sponsorship is not about profit. It is about cost recovery so this project can sustain itself and continue growing. Every textbook remains free under the CC BY-NC-SA 4.0 license, and sponsors receive recognition and tangible rewards rather than content influence.

    The strategy has three pillars:

    1. GitHub Sponsors for recurring individual and organizational support
    2. Axiom the Owl merchandise through a partnership with ShilpiWorks for custom laptop stickers and laser-cut bookmarks
    3. Conference and workshop bundles to spread awareness

    Pros and Cons with Objective Metrics

    Pros

    Pro Description Metric to Track Target
    Cost Coverage Offset AI image generation and API costs Monthly API spend vs. sponsorship revenue Revenue covers 80% of costs within 6 months
    Community Investment Sponsors become engaged contributors Sponsor-originated PRs and issues per quarter At least 3 per quarter
    Brand Awareness Axiom stickers spread visibility at conferences Items distributed per event; new GitHub stars within 30 days 50+ items per event; measurable star increase
    Educational Reach Sponsorship funds enable more textbooks New case study textbooks published per quarter Maintain or increase current pace
    Sustainability Reduces single-person dependency Active maintainers; financial runway in months 2+ maintainers; 3-month runway

    Cons

    Con Description Metric to Watch Trigger Threshold Mitigation
    Mission Drift Sponsors may expect content influence % of GitHub issues that are sponsor-driven feature requests >20% Publish editorial independence policy; redirect to feedback form
    Admin Overhead Managing sponsors, fulfilling merchandise, maintaining recognition page Hours per week on sponsor management >4 hours/week Automate with GitHub Actions; batch fulfillment quarterly
    Commercialization Perception May conflict with open education ethos Negative comments on LinkedIn/GitHub about commercialization 3+ sustained negative comments Add FAQ explaining cost-recovery; link to Mission and Values
    Fulfillment Complexity Physical goods require shipping and coordination Order-to-delivery time; support tickets about merchandise >3 weeks delivery or >5 tickets/month Shift to digital-only rewards
    Donor Fatigue Small projects see sponsor churn Monthly sponsor retention rate <70% for 3 consecutive months Offer annual sponsorship option; improve communication

    Revenue Streams

    Four Complementary Revenue Streams

    Each stream serves a different audience and timeline. GitHub Sponsors is the foundation; merchandise adds tangible value; conferences build awareness; grants are a long-term goal.

    1. GitHub Sponsors (Primary)

    • Individual sponsors at \(5-\)50/month tiers
    • Organizational sponsors at \(100-\)500/month
    • One-time contributions for milestones (e.g., "Fund the next 10 textbooks")
    • Requires creating .github/FUNDING.yml (does not exist yet)

    2. Axiom the Owl Merchandise via ShilpiWorks

    • Custom vinyl laptop stickers featuring all 8 Axiom emotional states
    • Laser-cut wood or acrylic bookmarks with Axiom silhouette
    • Per-textbook custom variants (70+ textbooks = 70+ unique Axiom designs)

    3. Conference and Workshop Bundles

    • Standard bundle: 5-sticker variety pack + bookmark + info card with QR code
    • Workshop kit: themed sticker matching workshop topic + bookmark + QR card
    • Target price: $5-10 per bundle to cover costs plus shipping

    4. Grant Funding (Long-term)

    • NSF grants for open educational technology
    • UNESCO OER funding opportunities
    • Foundation grants targeting educational equity
    • Note: Grant writing is time-intensive; this is a Phase 4+ activity

    ShilpiWorks Partnership

    Axiom Goes Physical

    ShilpiWorks creates products through a "human + machine" collaboration model, which mirrors how this project uses AI to create textbooks. A natural partnership.

    Partner Details

    • Website: shilpiworks.com
    • Contact: arun@netrii.com
    • Specialty: Vinyl stickers, laser-cut bookmarks, and art pieces
    • Philosophy: "Human + Machine" collaboration, aligning with this project's AI-assisted approach

    Axiom Sticker Line

    Stickers available in all 8 emotional states, matching the existing mascot assets in docs/img/mascot/:

    State Description Best For
    Welcome Wings open, greeting pose Conference giveaways
    Neutral Calm, steady presence General distribution
    Explain One wing raised, teaching Workshop participants
    Thinking Wing to chin, reflective Study group gifts
    Tip Pointing gesture Pro-tip reward
    Encourage Thumbs-up wing Sponsor thank-you
    Warning Cautionary pose Fun collector item
    Celebrate Wings spread, joyful Milestone rewards

    Sizes: Small (2"), Medium (3"), Large (4") - weather-resistant vinyl

    Laser-Cut Axiom Bookmarks

    • Wood or acrylic material with Axiom silhouette
    • Engraved with "Building Intelligent Textbooks" and site URL
    • Optional subject-specific text on reverse

    Per-Textbook Custom Variants

    The most distinctive part of this merchandise strategy. With 70+ case study textbooks, AI can generate subject-specific Axiom variants:

    • Axiom holding a qubit (quantum computing textbook)
    • Axiom with a geometry compass (geometry course)
    • Axiom at a circuit board (circuits course)
    • Axiom with a data chart (data science course)
    • Axiom examining DNA (bioinformatics course)

    This uses the same AI image generation workflow the project already uses for covers and mascot poses.

    Fulfillment Model

    • ShilpiWorks handles production
    • Dan coordinates orders
    • GitHub Sponsors tier fulfillment: collect shipping address in sponsor welcome flow
    • Conference orders: pre-order batch from ShilpiWorks 4-6 weeks in advance

    Sponsor Tiers

    Tiers Named After Axiom's States

    Each tier is named after one of Axiom's emotional states, creating a natural progression from reflection to celebration.

    Individual Tiers

    Tier Monthly Axiom State Benefits
    Thinking $5 Reflective (wing to chin) Name in SPONSORS.md; sponsor-only GitHub Discussions
    Encouraging $10 Thumbs-up wing Above + 1 Axiom laptop sticker (choice of pose) mailed annually
    Explaining $25 One wing raised, teaching Above + laser-cut Axiom bookmark
    Celebrating $50 Wings spread, joyful Above + full merch kit + name on sponsor recognition page + early access to new textbooks

    Organization Tiers

    Tier Monthly Benefits
    Supporting Org $100 Logo on sponsor page; 5 sticker packs for team; quarterly LinkedIn mention
    Champion Org $250 Above + co-branded Axiom variant sticker (Axiom with org logo)
    Founding Org $500 Above + named acknowledgment in all new textbooks; quarterly call with Dan; custom merch bundle for events

    Every Level Matters

    Even the $5 Thinking tier contributes meaningfully. A single AI image generation call costs \(0.02-\)0.10, so $5/month funds 50-250 generated images.

    Implementation Roadmap

    Phase 1: Foundation (Weeks 1-2)

    • [ ] Create .github/FUNDING.yml pointing to GitHub Sponsors
    • [ ] Set up GitHub Sponsors profile with tier descriptions and Axiom imagery
    • [ ] Write editorial independence policy
    • [ ] Add sponsorship link to Contact page and site footer

    Phase 2: Recognition Infrastructure (Weeks 3-4)

    • [ ] Create docs/sponsors.md recognition page
    • [ ] Add sponsor page to mkdocs.yml navigation
    • [ ] Design automated sponsor acknowledgment workflow
    • [ ] Announce sponsorship launch on LinkedIn

    Phase 3: ShilpiWorks Merchandise (Weeks 5-8)

    • [ ] Share mascot assets with ShilpiWorks for sticker design proofs
    • [ ] Get pricing for initial production run (100 stickers, 50 bookmarks)
    • [ ] Document order fulfillment process
    • [ ] Integrate merchandise into GitHub Sponsors tier descriptions

    Phase 4: Conference Bundles (Weeks 9-12)

    • [ ] Design conference bundle packaging
    • [ ] Generate per-textbook Axiom variants for top 5-10 textbooks
    • [ ] Test fulfillment process at first conference or workshop
    • [ ] Document process so volunteers can handle future events

    Phase 5: Scale and Optimize (Months 4-6)

    • [ ] Review all dashboard metrics
    • [ ] Adjust tiers based on actual sponsor distribution
    • [ ] Explore grant opportunities
    • [ ] Consider one-time contribution options for specific textbook projects

    Measurement Dashboard

    Are We Meeting Our Goals?

    Review these metrics monthly. Honest measurement prevents mission drift and ensures sponsorship remains a net positive.

    Monthly KPIs

    KPI How to Measure Target
    Monthly AI/API Costs Sum API invoices (OpenAI, Anthropic, image generation) Document baseline in Month 1
    Sponsorship Revenue GitHub Sponsors dashboard Cover 80% of costs by Month 6
    Total Sponsor Count GitHub Sponsors dashboard 20 sponsors by Month 6
    Sponsor Retention Rate (Retained / start of month) x 100 >75%
    New GitHub Stars (monthly) GitHub insights Maintain or increase growth rate
    Issue Sentiment Tag issues as sponsor-driven vs. organic <20% sponsor-driven
    Merchandise Orders ShilpiWorks order log 10+ orders/month by Month 6
    Fulfillment Time Date ordered to date shipped <2 weeks
    Admin Time Self-reported weekly log <2 hours/week
    Content Creation Time Self-reported weekly log No decrease from baseline
    LinkedIn Engagement Impressions and comments on sponsor announcements >80% positive sentiment

    Quarterly Review Process

    1. Update the scoring dashboard (green/yellow/red table)
    2. Post a brief public update for sponsors on GitHub Discussions or LinkedIn
    3. Adjust tiers or merchandise strategy based on data
    4. Go/no-go decision on advancing to next phase

    Risk Mitigation

    Managing Risks Proactively

    Each risk has a measurable trigger and a specific response. If thresholds are crossed, act immediately rather than waiting for the quarterly review.

    Risk Trigger Immediate Action Escalation
    Mission Drift >20% of issues are sponsor-driven Post editorial independence statement Pause org sponsorship tiers
    Admin Overload >4 hours/week on sponsor tasks Automate recognition page; batch fulfillment quarterly Simplify to 2 tiers; drop physical merch
    Commercialization Perception 3+ negative LinkedIn/GitHub comments Add FAQ on cost-recovery vs. profit; link to Mission and Values Rebrand as "sustainer" language
    Fulfillment Delays >3 weeks order-to-delivery Communicate delay; identify bottleneck Switch to digital-only rewards
    Low Uptake <10 sponsors after 3 months Increase LinkedIn promotion; create case study of what sponsorship funds Switch to one-time donation model (Ko-fi)
    License Conflict Any concern about CC BY-NC-SA 4.0 and sponsorship Review with community; NonCommercial applies to content, not project fundraising Publish explicit legal FAQ

    Decision Framework

    Pre-Launch Checklist

    All must be YES before proceeding:

    • [ ] Monthly AI/API costs documented for at least 2 months (baseline established)
    • [ ] .github/FUNDING.yml created and tested
    • [ ] GitHub Sponsors profile complete with tier descriptions and Axiom imagery
    • [ ] Editorial independence policy written and published
    • [ ] ShilpiWorks has confirmed pricing and timeline for initial batch
    • [ ] At least 3 trusted community members have reviewed this plan
    • [ ] Sponsor recognition page template is ready
    • [ ] Dan confirms willingness to commit up to 2 hours/week to management
    • [ ] Plan announced on LinkedIn to gauge community interest

    6-Month Review Checklist

    If fewer than 4 items are YES, scale back to GitHub Sponsors only (no merchandise) and re-evaluate in 3 months:

    • [ ] Sponsorship revenue covers at least 50% of documented costs
    • [ ] Sponsor count is at least 15
    • [ ] Admin time has not exceeded 4 hours/week in any month
    • [ ] No sustained negative community sentiment
    • [ ] Content creation pace has not decreased
    • [ ] At least one conference/workshop merchandise distribution completed

    Ready to Build Something Sustainable

    This plan balances open education values with practical sustainability. Every textbook stays free. Every dollar goes toward creating more of them.
